// Personal Background
MY STORY
From gears to gigabytes. A mechanical engineer who fell in love with how systems think — and spent a decade following that instinct across two countries.
2018 – 2021 B.E. Mechanical · 3rd Year

The detour that wasn't a detour.

I was three years into a Mechanical Engineering degree when I discovered something that most of my peers walked past: code has physics too. Systems have rules. Data centers have tolerances just like engines. I started learning programming and systems fundamentals not because it was required — but because I couldn't stop asking how things worked beneath the surface. That question has followed me everywhere since.

Jun 2021 – Jul 2022 ASBL · Hyderabad

My first data center — while still finishing my degree.

Campus placements came before graduation. I got placed as a Junior Data Center Technician at ASBL, Hyderabad in my final year and started working while completing my degree — a common path in India, and one that meant I entered the workforce with zero adjustment period. I learned that a data center isn't just hardware; it's a living system of power, cooling, cabling, and coordination where a single mishandled step cascades. I trained on rack and stack, structured cabling, and operations monitoring — and started building the instinct for thinking in systems, not tasks.

Jul 2022 – Aug 2023 Tata Communications · Hyderabad

Earned the next step. Through interviews, on merit.

When my contract at ASBL ended, I didn't wait for placement luck. I interviewed and earned a position as a Data Center Technician at Tata Communications — one of India's largest network infrastructure operators. Here, the scale was different. Cross-connects, enterprise clients, SLA-bound operations. I deepened my understanding of infrastructure at the tier where downtime has a dollar value. But alongside the hands-on work, I kept noticing something: the most interesting problems weren't in the racks — they were in the patterns the data was showing.

"I kept asking why operations decisions were made based on instinct when the data to make better ones was sitting right there."
Aug 2023 – Jun 2025 University of Dayton · Ohio, USA

First in my family. Across an ocean.

I applied for a Master's in Business Analytics (STEM) at the University of Dayton — and got in. This was not a small thing. I was the first person in my family to pursue graduate education, and the first to cross countries for it. I spent those two years learning what I hadn't had language for before: statistical modeling, data pipelines, visualization, business intelligence. Projects in Python, SQL, Power BI, Tableau — tools that let me finally do what I'd been wanting to do since Tata: turn operational data into decisions.

Aug 2025 – Present Tech Electronics · Ohio, USA

Where the threads finally met.

After graduating, I joined Tech Electronics as an IT Operations Analyst in Ohio — a role that sits exactly at the intersection of everything I'd built: infrastructure knowledge, systems thinking, and analytical capability. I've worked across IT operations, monitoring, and process improvement — translating the operational instincts I built in data centers into structured analytical work. The mechanical engineer, the DC tech, and the analytics graduate finally occupy the same job description.

Now

What's next.

My contract at Tech Electronics is winding down, and I'm actively looking for the next chapter — roles where operations and data live in the same room. Network Operations Centers, IT Operations Analyst positions, Data Center Operations, or anything where monitoring systems, diagnosing patterns, and keeping infrastructure running cleanly is the job. I bring something most analysts don't: I've worked the floor. I know what the alerts mean because I've traced the cable behind them.
